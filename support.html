FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Why are my prayer times different from another app? ▾

Prayer time calculations depend on three things: the solar algorithm, the calculation method (Fajr/Isha angles), and whether DST is handled correctly for the target date rather than the current date. Different apps use different implementations of the same formulas, which can produce times that differ by a few minutes.

Iqamah uses the NOAA/USNO solar position algorithm with DST-aware timezone offsets. If you notice a systematic difference (same city, same method, every day), please open an issue with the city, date, method, and the times from both apps.

The app shows the wrong city after I move. ▾

Iqamah saves your selected city in preferences — it does not automatically update when you travel. To change your location:

1. Click the menu bar icon → open the main window.
2. Go to Location in the navigation.
3. Either tap Auto-detect to use your current GPS position, or search for your new city manually.

Which calculation method should I use? ▾

Use the method endorsed by the Islamic authority most relevant to your region:

MWL — Muslim World League. Widely used in Europe, Far East, parts of US.
ISNA — Islamic Society of North America. Common in North America.
Egypt — Egyptian General Authority of Survey. Africa, Syria, Lebanon, Malaysia.
Umm Al-Qura — Official Saudi Arabian times. Arabian Peninsula.
Karachi — Pakistan, Afghanistan, India, Bangladesh.
Tehran — Institute of Geophysics, University of Tehran. Iran, some Shia communities.

For Asr: Standard (Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali) uses shadow factor 1; Hanafi uses shadow factor 2, giving a later Asr time.

The menu bar countdown stopped updating. ▾

The countdown refreshes every 60 seconds. If it appears frozen, quit and relaunch Iqamah from the Applications folder. If the issue persists after relaunch, please report it on GitHub — include your macOS version and whether the freeze happens at a specific time of day (e.g. at midnight when prayer times roll over).

How do I reset the app to its default settings? ▾

Quit Iqamah, then run this command in Terminal to clear all saved preferences:

defaults delete com.iqamah.app

Relaunch the app — the setup wizard will appear as if it's a fresh install.

Does Iqamah work without an internet connection? ▾

Yes, fully. All prayer times are calculated using an on-device astronomical algorithm. The cities database (559 cities, 55 countries) is bundled inside the app. Iqamah makes zero network requests — it works completely offline, on a plane, or behind a firewall.

My city isn't in the list. ▾

The current database includes 559 cities across 55 countries, covering most major population centres. If your city is missing, two options:

1. Use Auto-detect — Iqamah will use your GPS coordinates directly and find the nearest city for timezone purposes. Prayer times will still be accurate for your exact location.

2. Request the city on GitHub — include the city name, country, latitude, longitude, and IANA timezone identifier (e.g. America/Chicago).

Known Issues

Current status

Issues we're tracking, actively fixing, or have already resolved in the latest version.

Fixed
Prayer times shift by 1 hour across DST boundary
Times calculated in winter for a summer date (or vice versa) were off by one hour. Fixed in v1.0 — the calculator now uses the DST offset for the target date, not the current date.
Known
Fajr / Isha times differ from IslamicFinder by up to ~20 min
Iqamah uses the NOAA solar algorithm with official MWL angles (Fajr 18°, Isha 17°). Some other calculators use adjusted angles (~15°) which produce different results. Both are valid implementations of MWL — there is no single authoritative reference. An accuracy review is planned.
Known
VoiceOver navigation not fully labelled
A full VoiceOver accessibility audit is in progress (GitHub issue #3). Some interactive elements may lack descriptive labels. Fix targeted for v1.1.
Bug Reports

How to report a bug

A good bug report helps us fix the issue faster. Follow these steps before opening a GitHub issue:

1
Check the known issues list above
If your bug is already listed, you can add a comment to the existing GitHub issue with your reproduction steps — it helps us prioritise.
2
Note your macOS version and app version
Open Apple menu → About This Mac for macOS. The Iqamah version appears in Iqamah → About Iqamah in the menu bar.
3
Write clear reproduction steps
Include: what you did, what you expected to happen, and what actually happened. For prayer time issues, include city name, calculation method, Asr setting, and the date.
4
Open an issue on GitHub
Go to github.com/ksyed0/iqamah/issues/new and paste your report. Screenshots are welcome. You do not need a GitHub account to view issues, but you do need one to submit.
✦  Prefer email?

If you'd rather not use GitHub, email a description to ksyed0@gmail.com and we'll log it for you.
