## Privacy Nutrition Labels (App Privacy Section in App Store Connect)

Navigate to **App Privacy** in App Store Connect and answer:

### Does your app collect data?
**→ No** — Select "We do not collect data from this app."

This covers:
- No contact info collected
- No health or fitness data
- No financial info
- No location data transmitted (location used on-device only, not collected)
- No sensitive info
- No contacts, photos, or files
- No usage data
- No diagnostics or crash data
- No other data

> **Note on Location:** Although Iqamah accesses device location, it is used **only on-device** and is **not** collected, stored outside the device, or linked to the user's identity. Under Apple's definition, data that stays entirely on the device and is not sent to Apple or any third party does **not** need to be declared as "collected." If Apple's review team asks for clarification, state: "Location data is used at runtime to find the nearest city from a local database. It is not stored, transmitted, or linked to any identifier."

## Notes for App Review

Include in your **App Review Notes** when submitting:

> Iqamah is an Islamic prayer times calculator for macOS. It requests location permission to automatically find the user's nearest city from a local database bundled with the app. No data is transmitted to any server. The app can be used fully without granting location permission by selecting a city manually from the country/city dropdowns.
>
> The splash screen on first launch shows a message from the developer asking users to donate to charity as a form of payment — this is a personal request, not a solicitation or in-app purchase mechanism.
>
> Test account: Not required — no login or account needed.
