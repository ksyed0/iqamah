✦  Bottom line

Iqamah does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything the app needs to function lives entirely on your device and never leaves it.

Section 01

Information we do not collect

Iqamah has no servers, no database, and no analytics pipeline. The following are explicitly not collected, transmitted, or processed by this app:

Personal identifiers
Device ID or advertising ID
Location sent to any server
Usage analytics
Crash reports
Email or contact info
Section 02

Location data

Iqamah requests permission to access your device's location only to identify the nearest city from its built-in database and calculate accurate Islamic prayer times. This happens entirely on-device:

Your coordinates are used in memory at runtime only. No location history is stored. No coordinates are ever sent off your Mac.

If you deny location permission, you can still use the app fully by selecting your city manually from the built-in list of 559 cities across 55 countries.

✦  On-device only

All prayer time calculations use Apple's CoreLocation API on your device. Your location never travels over any network connection.

Section 03

Data stored on your device

Iqamah saves a small set of preferences using Apple's UserDefaults system. This data lives in the macOS App Sandbox on your Mac and is never transmitted anywhere.

Data Purpose
Selected city name & coordinates Calculate prayer times for your location
Calculation method Determine Fajr and Isha angles (e.g. MWL, ISNA)
Asr jurisprudence Standard or Hanafi shadow factor
Per-prayer minute adjustments Show your preferred adjusted times
Setup completion flag Skip onboarding on subsequent launches

All of this data is deleted permanently when you uninstall the app.

Section 04

Third-party services

Iqamah integrates no third-party SDKs, analytics libraries, advertising networks, crash reporters, or cloud services. The app makes zero network requests of any kind during normal operation.

The app is distributed through the Mac App Store. Apple's own App Store terms and privacy policy apply to the download and purchase process, which is handled entirely by Apple.

Section 05

Children's privacy

Iqamah does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Because no data is collected at all, there is no risk of inadvertent collection from any age group.

Section 06

Data security

Because Iqamah collects no data and makes no network connections, there is no data transmission to secure. All preferences are stored inside Apple's macOS App Sandbox using standard UserDefaults, subject to the full security protections of macOS.

Section 07

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a new effective date. Given that the app collects no data, material changes are unlikely. The current effective date is shown in the page header.
